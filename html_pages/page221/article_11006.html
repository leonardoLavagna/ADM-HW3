Synopsis

We follow the daily events of Pac as he saves Pac-World. Together with his friends Cyli and Spiral, Pac and his buds face the issues of being teenagers while also protecting Pac-World from a ghostly army led by the evil Betrayus. Pac-Worlders do not want Betrayus and his ghostly minions returning from the Netherworld and wreaking havoc. So, President Spheros teaches Pac and his pals the legend prophesizing a round yellow Pac-Worlder rescuing the planet. Pac, the only round yellow citizen, is the man for the job. Each adventure follows Pac and his friends as they try to return the ghosts to Netherworld, saving Pac-World.

(Source: AniDB)

Honestly, I can’t even remember if I actually finished this show, but I’m going to review it anyway because I will never watch it again. This review is a matured version of the review I wrote on my old account, mothersSPAGHETTI. And may I comment that I’m quite baffled this is on here, because the show was first aired in 2013 in Canada and America before even coming to Japan.

Of course, this is related to Pac-Man, one of my favorite arcade games of all time (besides Dig Dug, Galaga, Centipede, to name a few), so I thought I’d like it. Honestly. I loved watching this show when I was 12 years old. However, the reason I dislike it now is for a personal reason, but I’ll be as honest as I can in this review as to why I dislike it now.

The story doesn’t sound interesting written on paper, and neither does it sound interesting seen on screen. For whatever reason, I liked this then, but looking back at it now, this entire show is aged poorly. It’s filled to the brim with unfunny wordplays, jokes that try way to hard to be hip, and burp + fart jokes galore. This is humor only a 10 year old should laugh at.

The CGI is surprisingly decent. While it has WAY too many bright colors, it’s definitely the best aspect of this show by far and the characters actually show emotion. I do enjoy the animation and CGI in this show as it isn’t particularly the worst. Also, if this is the same OLM that animates Pokemon and the upcoming Komi-san anime, I will give them credit for trying.

The sound is okay. I have no clue what the Japanese audio sounds like because I’ve only watched it in English; But the voices fit the characters decently. The music however, gets on my nerves and is sometimes slapped into the scenes at the worst times. As for openings, the Japanese OP by Hyadain (who does the ED theme for Nyanpire) is kinda catchy. However, the English OP tries way too hard to be hip and the guy singing sounds like he’s trying not to cry the whole time while singing. He also sings off-key which makes it worse.

The characters are pretty two-dimensional. My boy Pac-Man (Pac or Pacster as his cardboard cutout “friends” refer to him as) has been dumbed down and is now a complete nobody who burps all the time. Cylindria, or whatever her name is, is literally the “smart one” of the group but is boring as hell. Then there’s Spiral, who’s just as dumb as Pac-Man if not worse, except the only difference is that he’s sporty. The ghosts are friends with “Pac-Man” (if I should even call him that) for whatever reason. Pinky having a crush on Pac-Man pisses me off most. Betrayus (Most idiotic name for a character I have ever heard) is literally the worst villain I have ever seen in TV show history. He throws fits whenever he doesn’t get his way and acts like a 4 year old. Then there’s the twin ghosts with the fucking ass cheeks on their foreheads. Their names?? Butt-ler and Dr. Buttocks. Gee whiz, so fucking hilarious. Again, humor only a 10 year old should laugh at. The only character I like is the small orange dude with the big ears (he’s like a Pokemon-ish thing). Everyone else is a boring stereotype.


I could not get myself to actually enjoy this show. As hard as I try, I can’t. I think I will stick to playing the arcade title (and the one I have for DS), but I cannot enjoy this as watching this show hurts my brain. It doesn’t even bother me that much that it isn’t faithful to its source material, (Though I feel like this show is a before-it-all-started type deal).

I would not recommend this show. But if you’d like to give it a try, hey, be my guest. There’s a game to go with this show too, it’s better than show, but it’s still not good. Also speaking of my old review, I wouldn’t exactly call this show “garbage” as there are definitely better things to watch out there, though I would still definitely recommend watching other shows from OLM or OLM Digital.

This is not a good show. Not even a decent one. Instead of staying faithful to the source material, like other video game adaptions such as Mario and Sonic did, they went in a completely new direction and ripped off several shows in the process.

This is a prequel to the Pac-Man games, revolving around Pac-Man's teenager days instead of his usual adult self. The story is pretty much like Ben 10, but with ghosts. Pac-Man eats pellets with different super powers, but he never bothers to learn what each of them do. An evil ghost sends out other ghosts to get him, but fails every time. Pac-Man doesn't even need to take a power pill before eating ghosts, he can just eat them whenever he wants with no penality.

The characters are all stereotypical as well. Pac-Man acts like a teenager with an attitude (e.g. annoying), and he has a "smart" female Pac-friend for girl power reasons. And, of course, your usual sports jock shaped as a... bean? They also have a dog named "Packster" for comic relief. Then his ghostly friends... A stupid red ghost, a pink ghost in love with him. And, of course, there's a stereotypical bad guy who keeps sending ghosts after Pac-Man and his friends.

The music, however, is pretty good. It has a very "movie-esque" feel, with orchestrated themes and remixes of old music tracks from the earlier games. The English intro is one of the worst I've ever heard, with off-key singing and a slow-paced techno theme. But the Japanese version was amazing and really gets you pumped up for a very mediocre show!

The CG animation is also pretty good. The characters are full of facial expressions, they move around a lot and the frame rate is very smooth. However, the backgrounds are rather bland, as everything looks circular and like it was made out of plastic. Same goes for the props. Not many details either, as the town is pretty empty.

It's pretty much like a bad saturday morning cartoon from the 70's. Predictable plot, horrible bathroom jokes + unfunny word play, and a cast with mostly bland characters you've seen everywhere else. There is probably some enjoyment out of this for kids, but older people will hardly find anything to enjoy. There's not even any innuendo...
